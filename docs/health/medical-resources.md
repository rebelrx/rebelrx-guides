## 🧪 Comprehensive Annual Lab Panel (Baseline)

> ⚠️ Labs are only as useful as their interpretation.  
> Focus on trends over time, not single values.

---

### 🩸 Basic Health and Organ Function

**Why it matters:**  
Establishes a baseline for organ function, blood health, and general physiology.

- Comprehensive Metabolic Panel (CMP)  
- Complete Blood Count (CBC) with differential  
- Lipid Panel  
  - Total cholesterol  
  - LDL-C  
  - HDL-C  
  - Triglycerides  

---

### ❤️ Cardiometabolic Risk

**Why it matters:**  
Identifies early drivers of heart disease and metabolic dysfunction, often years before symptoms.

- Apolipoprotein B (ApoB) → best single marker for cardiovascular risk  
- Lipoprotein(a) [Lp(a)] → genetically driven risk factor  
- High-sensitivity C-reactive protein (hs-CRP) → systemic inflammation  
- Fasting insulin → early insulin resistance (often missed)  
- Hemoglobin A1c  
- Fasting glucose  

> 💡 Tip: Triglyceride-to-HDL ratio is a powerful proxy for metabolic health.

---

### 🔥 Inflammation and Immune Activity

**Why it matters:**  
Chronic inflammation is a root driver of many diseases, including cardiovascular, metabolic, and autoimmune conditions.

- Erythrocyte sedimentation rate (ESR)  
- Ferritin → also reflects inflammation, not just iron status  

---

### ⚡ Thyroid Panel (Comprehensive)

**Why it matters:**  
Evaluates metabolism, energy production, and hormonal balance beyond basic screening.

- Thyroid-stimulating hormone (TSH)  
- Free T4  
- Free T3  
- Reverse T3  
- Thyroid peroxidase antibodies (TPO Ab)  
- Thyroglobulin antibodies (Tg Ab)  

---

### 🧬 Hormonal Panel

**Why it matters:**  
Assesses endocrine function, recovery, stress response, and overall vitality.

#### Men

- Total testosterone  
- Free testosterone  
- Sex hormone-binding globulin (SHBG)  
- Estradiol (sensitive assay)  
- Dehydroepiandrosterone sulfate (DHEA-S)  

#### Women

- Estradiol  
- Progesterone  
- Follicle-stimulating hormone (FSH)  
- Luteinizing hormone (LH)  
- Total and free testosterone  
- Dehydroepiandrosterone sulfate (DHEA-S)  

---

### 🧱 Nutrient and Deficiency Markers

**Why it matters:**  
Identifies common deficiencies that impact energy, cognition, and long-term health.

- Vitamin D (25-OH)  
- Vitamin B12  
- Folate  
- Iron panel  
  - Serum iron  
  - Total iron-binding capacity (TIBC)  
  - Transferrin saturation  
  - Ferritin  
- Magnesium (RBC magnesium preferred if available)  

---

### 🧪 Additional Metabolic Markers

**Why it matters:**  
Provides deeper insight into oxidative stress, detoxification capacity, and metabolic health.

- Gamma-glutamyl transferase (GGT)  
- Uric acid  

---

### 🧠 Advanced / Optional (Case-Dependent)

**Why it matters:**  
Used for deeper investigation when abnormalities are present or optimization is the goal.

#### Metabolic

- C-peptide  
- Advanced lipid panel (LDL-P via NMR)  
- Omega-3 Index  

#### Inflammation / Methylation

- Homocysteine  

#### Hormonal / Endocrine

- Cortisol (AM ± PM)  
- Prolactin  

#### Autoimmune (if indicated)

- Antinuclear antibody (ANA)  

---

## 🧭 Tiered Approach

Start simple. Expand based on findings.

### Tier 1 (Core – Everyone)

- CBC, CMP, Lipid panel  
- Hemoglobin A1c, fasting insulin  
- ApoB, hs-CRP  
- TSH, Free T4  
- Vitamin D  

---

### Tier 2 (If abnormalities or optimization focus)

- Lipoprotein(a)  
- Free T3  
- Iron panel + ferritin  
- Vitamin B12, folate  
- GGT  

---

### Tier 3 (Targeted / case-dependent)

- Hormones (sex hormones, DHEA-S)  
- Reverse T3  
- C-peptide  
- Homocysteine  
- Autoimmune markers (e.g., ANA)
